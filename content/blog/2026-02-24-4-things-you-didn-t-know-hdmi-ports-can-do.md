HDMI ports have become a staple in modern entertainment systems, allowing us to connect our devices to TVs, projectors, and other displays with ease. However, many of us are not aware of the full potential of these ports. From enhanced video modes to high-quality audio, HDMI ports can do more than just transmit video and audio signals. In this article, we will explore four things you didn't know HDMI ports can do, and how they can enhance your viewing and gaming experience.

## 🔍 What Happened?
The development of HDMI technology has been ongoing for several years, with new features and capabilities being added to each successive version. The latest HDMI 2.1 standard, for example, supports higher resolutions, faster refresh rates, and more advanced audio formats. One of the key features of HDMI 2.1 is its ability to support 4K resolution at 120Hz with Variable Refresh Rate (VRR), making it ideal for gaming and other high-speed applications. Additionally, HDMI 2.1 also supports enhanced Audio Return Channel (eARC), which allows for higher-quality audio to be transmitted from a TV to a soundbar or home theater system.

## ⚡ Why This Matters
The enhanced capabilities of HDMI ports matter because they can significantly improve the viewing and gaming experience. For example, the ability to switch to auto game mode automatically when a game console is connected can reduce input lag and provide a more responsive gaming experience. Similarly, the support for 4K at 120Hz with VRR can provide a smoother and more immersive experience, especially in fast-paced games. The support for eARC, on the other hand, can provide higher-quality audio, making it ideal for home theater systems and soundbars.
