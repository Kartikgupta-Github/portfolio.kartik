## 🔍 What Happened?
To understand the making of the music of Kirby Air Ride, we need to go back to the game's development process. According to Masahiro Sakurai, the idea for Kirby Air Ride was born out of a desire to create a game that was both fun and easy to play. 'We wanted to make a game that anyone could pick up and enjoy, regardless of their skill level,' Sakurai explains. 'We experimented with different gameplay mechanics, and eventually settled on a racing game that featured Kirby and his friends.' With the gameplay in place, the team turned their attention to the music, bringing in renowned composer Noriyuki Iwadare to create the soundtrack.

## ⚡ Why This Matters
So why is the music of Kirby Air Ride so important? For Noriyuki Iwadare, the answer is simple: 'Music has the power to elevate a game from mere entertainment to an emotional experience.' Iwadare's approach to composing the soundtrack was to create music that was both catchy and unique, with a focus on capturing the lighthearted, carefree spirit of the Kirby series. 'I wanted to create music that would make players feel happy and excited, with a sense of freedom and joy,' Iwadare explains. The result is a soundtrack that is both nostalgic and innovative, with a unique blend of electronic and orchestral elements that perfectly complements the game's fast-paced action.
