In a surprising turn of events, DJI has agreed to pay $30,000 to a man who accidentally hacked into a network of 7,000 robot vacuums. The incident has raised eyebrows in the tech community, sparking discussions about the importance of cybersecurity in connected devices. The man, Sammy Azdoufal, used a tool called Claude Code to gain access to the network, highlighting the vulnerabilities that exist in many smart devices.

## 🔍 What Happened?
Sammy Azdoufal, a user of the Claude Code tool, stumbled upon a vulnerability that allowed him to access a network of 7,000 Romo robot vacuums. The incident was accidental, and Azdoufal did not intend to cause any harm. However, the fact that he was able to access such a large number of devices raises concerns about the security of connected devices. The Romo robot vacuums are not DJI products, but the company has agreed to pay Azdoufal $30,000 for his discovery.
