Samsung is on the hunt for artificial intelligence deals to challenge Apple's lead in the smartphone market. The Korean giant's device chief has revealed that its future Galaxy devices will host multiple AI models, giving users the freedom to mix and match AI tools to suit their needs. This move is seen as a strategic attempt to regain ground in the highly competitive smartphone market, where Apple has been dominating with its sleek designs and user-friendly interfaces.

## 🔍 What Happened?
Samsung's device chief, TM Roh, announced that the company is planning to introduce multiple AI models in its future Galaxy devices. This means that users will be able to choose from a range of AI tools and customize their smartphone experience according to their preferences. The move is seen as a bold attempt by Samsung to challenge Apple's dominance in the smartphone market, where the iPhone has been the top-selling device for years. Samsung has been struggling to keep up with Apple's pace, despite its efforts to innovate and introduce new features in its Galaxy series.

The introduction of multiple AI models in Samsung's future Galaxy devices is a significant development that could potentially disrupt the smartphone market. Moreover, the move is seen as a strategic attempt by Samsung to differentiate itself from Apple, which has been criticized for its closed ecosystem and limited customization options.
