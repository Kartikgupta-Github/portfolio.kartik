## What Actually Happened
The introduction of Digital ID in Apple's Wallet app last fall was a significant step forward in digital identification. Initially, it allowed users to store their state-issued IDs in a digital format, aiming to reduce the need for physical wallets. The latest expansion of this feature to include age verification is a natural progression, leveraging the security and convenience of digital identities to facilitate age-sensitive transactions or interactions. This could range from online purchases of age-restricted products to entry into events or venues where age verification is required.

## Technical Breakdown
From a technical standpoint, integrating Apple's Digital ID for age verification involves understanding how the Wallet app interacts with your application. This typically requires:
1. **Checking Compatibility**: Ensuring your application is compatible with the latest version of iOS and the Wallet app.
2. **Implementing Age Verification Requests**: Your app needs to request age verification from the user, which can then be fulfilled using the Digital ID if the user has it set up.
3. **Handling Verification Responses**: Developing your application to handle the response from the Wallet app, whether the verification is successful or not, and acting accordingly.
